FAQ

Who are you people?

The BRIGHT CANOPY project is a lean startup in Grass Valley, California launched as part of the Stanford, Startup Engineering project driven by Balaji S. Srinivasan and Vijay S. Pande. Mr Srinivasan and Mr. Pande are not otherwise involved with BRIGHT CANOPY. Our founder is Bill Glover who has been around the block a time or two and is a Virtual World native.

What will this money be used for?

The money from this starter will be used to pay for the servers, licenses and development time it will take to build out the rest of the features.

What do I get when I buy land?

This is a starter to get things rolling. It's a chance for pioneering folks and would-be land barrons to grab land early before anyone else hears about us. Your early support earns you an advantage in price and choice. We will be strictly first-come, first-serve. Each land purchase will be recorded and appear on The Big Map as well as the BRIGHT CANOPY channel in the Junaio Augmented Reality browser. You will be able to walk around and see your marker and those of others nearby floating in space. You will be able to name your land and set the description as well as an optional web link. Once we rollout user created content, land owners will be able to set permissions on who can create content (3D objects, flying monkeys, rotating billboards with spotlights and blink tags etc..) on your land. Once we rollout the land market, land owners will be able to buy and sell land.

Who owns the content I create in the canopy?

Creators retain all rights to the objects they create. We will only ask you for the rights we will need to display your objects and allow others to interact with them when you place them out to share with others. You will have the ability to sell copies of your items and control (as much as is technically feasible) the ability of others to make copies within the canopy.

Why Bitcoin?

Bitcoin is an international currency with quite a few properties that make it perfect for use in an virtual world. For a great overview see A Beginner's Guide to Bitcoin.
