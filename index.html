FAQ

What will this money be used for?

The money from this starter will be used to pay for the servers, licenses and development time it will take to build out the rest of the features.

What do I get when I buy land?

This is a starter to get things rolling. It's a chance for pioneering folks and would-be land barrons to grab land early before anyone else hears about us. Your early support earns you an advantage in price and choice. We will be strictly first-come, first-serve.

Each land purchase will be recorded and appear on The Big Map as well as the BRIGHT CANOPY channel in the Junaio Augmented Reality browser. You will be able to walk around and see your marker and those of others nearby floating in space. You will be able to name your land and set the description as well as an optional web link.

Once we rollout user created content, land owners will be able to set permissions on who can create content (3D objects, flying monkeys, rotating billboards with spotlights and blink tags etc..) on your land.

Once we rollout the land market, land owners will be able to buy and sell land.

Who owns the content I create in the canopy?

Creators retain all rights to the objects they create. We will only ask for the minimum rights necessary to do what you want us to do with your objects. You will have the ability to sell copies of your items and control (as much as is technically feasible) the ability of others to make copies within the canopy.

What are the risks and challenges?

Augmented Reality is a fast developing field again. The hardware and infrastructure is just starting to catch up with our childhood expectations, but it is catching up and most of the things we want to do have already been built, debugged and packaged into a nice, neat SDK or cloud API.

What we Know: We know we can do Augmented Reality with 3D, scriptable objects. We know we can do user created content. We know we can build land auctions, vending machines and virtual stores based on bitcoin. It's just work and time and money.

What We Don't Know: We don't know if you will be excited enough about this idea to get out there and invite your friends to help build a new world. If you are, we can do anything.

Who are you people?

The BRIGHT CANOPY project is a lean startup in Grass Valley, California launched as part of the Stanford, Startup Engineering project.

Startup Engineering is a class taught by Balaji S. Srinivasan and Vijay S. Pande. This project is a direct result of their inspiration and encouragement. Mr. Srinivasan and Mr. Pande are not otherwise involved with BRIGHT CANOPY. (If we screw-up it's not their fault, they've given great direction).

Our founder is Bill Glover who has been around the block a time or two and is a Virtual World native.

Why Bitcoin?

Bitcoin is an international currency with properties that make it work well for a startup and especially well for a virtual world virtual world. For a great overview see A Beginner's Guide to Bitcoin.

So it's a Junaio channel?

Junaio has some nice features, but it's just a good place to start for now. It doesn't have all of the features we will eventually need, so we expect to move on to a custom application before long. The idea is to learn as quickly as possibly, what you, the creator, resident or explorer really want and build just that without alot of cruft and waste.

So it's Second Life?

Second Life is an amazing thing and Linden Labs and Phillip Rosedale really blazed a trail for the rest of us, but it's a virtual world off in its own virtual space somewhere.

BRIGHT CANOPY is an augmented reality layer over the world you already know. You can build a copy of the Parthenon in SL with a guided tour and even build a souvenier stand to sell scale models (even full size), but in the canopy, you can do all of that at the actual Parthenon. Even better you can do all of that in your own travel agency office in Topeka, KS.
